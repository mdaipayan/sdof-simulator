PHASE 1: The Setup

Eq. of Motion: \(M\ddot{x} + C\dot{x} + Kx = F(t)\)

💡 Beginner Intuition: This equation just means: (Inertia) + (Friction) + (Spring Force) = (Outside Push). It is just Newton's Second Law (\(F=ma\)) expanded!
  1. Find \(M\) and \(K\). If \(K\) is missing, calculate it via static deflection (\(K = Mg/\delta_{st}\)) or beam formula (\(3EI/L^3\)).
  2. Calculate the Holy Trinity:
    • Nat. Freq: \(\omega_n = \sqrt{K/M}\)
    • Crit. Damping: \(C_c = 2\sqrt{KM}\)
    • Damp. Ratio: \(\zeta = C / C_c\)

PHASE 2: Choose Your Path

  • If there is NO motor, NO harmonic force, and NO unbalance: FREE VIBRATION → Move to 2A.
    \(F(t) = 0 \implies M\ddot{x} + C\dot{x} + Kx = 0\)
  • If there is a driving force \(F(t)\) or an unbalanced rotating mass: FORCED VIBRATION → Move to 2B.
    \(F(t) = F_0\sin(\omega t)\)

PHASE 2A: Complete Free Solutions

  1. Check \(\zeta\) to pick your base equation:
    • \(\zeta = 0 \implies\) Undamped
      \(x(t) = A\cos(\omega_n t) + B\sin(\omega_n t)\)
      Derivative: \(v(t) = -A\omega_n\sin(\omega_n t) + B\omega_n\cos(\omega_n t)\)
      → \(B = v_0 / \omega_n\)
    • \(0 < \zeta < 1 \implies\) Underdamped (\(\omega_d = \omega_n\sqrt{1-\zeta^2}\))
      \(x(t) = e^{-\zeta\omega_n t}(A\cos\omega_d t + B\sin\omega_d t)\)
      Derivative: \(v(t) = e^{-\zeta\omega_n t}[(-\zeta\omega_n A + \omega_d B)\cos\omega_d t - (\zeta\omega_n B + \omega_d A)\sin\omega_d t]\)
      → \(B = (v_0 + \zeta\omega_n A) / \omega_d\)
    • \(\zeta = 1 \implies\) Critically Damped
      \(x(t) = (A + Bt)e^{-\omega_n t}\)
      Derivative: \(v(t) = [B - \omega_n(A + Bt)]e^{-\omega_n t}\)
      → \(B = v_0 + \omega_n A\)
    2. 💡 Beginner Intuition (Constants A & B):
    The equations above are "generic". To make them fit our specific problem, we must apply our starting rules.
    1. Set \(t=0\) in the displacement equation to find \(A\).
    2. Take the derivative to get the velocity equation, then set \(t=0\) to find \(B\).
  2. Step 1: Plug \(t=0\) and \(x(0) = x_0\) into the displacement equation to find constant \(A\) (\(A = x_0\)).
  3. Step 2: Plug \(t=0\) and \(v(0) = v_0\) into the derivative equation to find constant \(B\) using the derived formulas above.

PHASE 2B: Complete Forced Solutions

  1. Calculate Freq Ratio: \(r = \omega / \omega_n\).
  2. Calculate Steady Amplitude (\(X\)) and Phase Angle (\(\phi\)):
    • \(X = \frac{F_0 / K}{\sqrt{(1 - r^2)^2 + (2\zeta r)^2}}\)
    • \(\phi = \tan^{-1}\left( \frac{2\zeta r}{1 - r^2} \right)\)
  3. ⚠️ TRAP: If \(r > 1\), add \(180^\circ\) to your calculator's \(\phi\)! Conversion: \(\text{Radians} = \text{Degrees} \times \frac{\pi}{180}\)
  4. Steady State Only:
    \(x_p(t) = X\sin(\omega t - \phi)\)
    5. 💡 Beginner Intuition (The "Gap"):
    The Steady-State wave (\(x_p\)) has a life of its own. At time \(t=0\), it is NOT at your starting position \(x_0\).
    The "Transient" equation (\(x_c\)) acts as a temporary mathematical bridge to connect your actual starting point to the steady-state wave.
  5. Complete Solution (Transient + Steady State):
    • Undamped + Forced:
      \(x(t) = [A\cos(\omega_n t) + B\sin(\omega_n t)] + X\sin(\omega t - \phi)\)
    • Underdamped + Forced:
      \(x(t) = e^{-\zeta\omega_n t}(A\cos\omega_d t + B\sin\omega_d t) + X\sin(\omega t - \phi)\)
    • Critically Damped + Forced:
      \(x(t) = [(A + Bt)e^{-\omega_n t}] + X\sin(\omega t - \phi)\)
  6. Finding A and B for Forced Systems:
    • Disp. gap at \(t=0\): \( x_{trans}(0) = x_0 - X\sin(-\phi) \)
      → This equals \(A\).
    • Vel. gap at \(t=0\): \( v_{trans}(0) = v_0 - \omega X\cos(-\phi) \)
      → Use this "transient initial velocity" in the standard Phase 2A derivative formulas to find \(B\).
